Interesting facts about Camel


  • Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day. Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat. Camels humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

Interesting facts about Chameleon


  • "Chameleon" Means "Ground Lion". Almost Half the Population Lives in Madagascar. Most of the Chameleons have Prehensile Tails. Chameleons mainly change colour in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.

Interesting facts about Pigeon


  • Pigeons can fly at the altitude of 6000 feet. Pigeons can move their wings ten times per second and maintain heartbeats at the rate of 600 times per minute, during the period of 16 hours. Pigeons can fly at the speed of 50 to 60 miles per hour. Scientific name of pigeon is "Columbidae".

Interesting facts about Teddy Bear


  • The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846. The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906. In 1902 in Germany, Steiff launched “Bear 55 PB”, the first toy bear with jointed arms and legs.

Interesting facts about Panda


  • The giant panda's distinct black-and-white markings have two functions: camouflage and communication. Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo. Pandas need at least 2 different bamboo species in their range to avoid starvation. The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.

Interesting facts about Cicada


  • Cicadas can be black, brown or green and can have red, white or blue eyes. Cicadas wings are transparent and can seem rainbow-hued when held up to a light source. The body of a cicada is similar to that of a violin or a guitar, in that much of it consists of empty, air-filled spaces that act like a resonating chamber and amplify the sound they generate. Cicadas make noise like toy frogs.
